# DevWhateverOps

**Breaking things carefully is how you go faster.**

A manifesto for engineers who build end to end — written as the AI hype begins to settle.

→ [devwhateverops.com](https://www.devwhateverops.com)

-----

## What This Is

DevWhateverOps is a set of principles for engineering culture at the intersection of Dev, Ops, Security, AI, and everything in between.

Not a framework. Not a methodology. Not a certification.

A point of view — on how modern engineering teams should think about ownership, pipelines, and shipping with confidence.

The manifesto is built around three pillars:

- **Culture** — Own the full loop. No gates, no handoffs, no role hiding.
- **Pipeline** — Automate everything. Smooth is fast, fast is smooth.
- **Experiments** — Every change is a hypothesis. Ship small, observe, decide.

-----

## Why This Exists

Every “-Ops” suffix we invented — DevOps, SecOps, AIOps, DocOps — was an attempt to name a culture shift. Most of the time it became a job title instead. A new silo built to solve the problem of silos.

This manifesto argues for something simpler: engineers who own the full loop, pipelines that run without human intervention, and a culture where every change is an experiment and breaking things carefully is how you move fast.

It is written now, as AI hype begins to settle, because the fundamentals it describes are not new. AI is just making the cost of ignoring them more visible and more painful.

Contributions are welcome. A few guidelines:

- **Issues** — for challenging a principle, proposing a new one, or pointing out something that doesn’t hold up
- **Pull requests** — for specific, concrete improvements to the text
- **Discussions** — for broader conversation about where this is heading

This is not a democracy. It is a maintained point of view. PRs may be declined if they change the direction rather than sharpen it.
